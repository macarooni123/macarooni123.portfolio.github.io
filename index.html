My experience and progress during 2019/2020

My Modules

Intro to JS

Intro to JS used Javascript to make pictures and drawings. This was a fun way to learn JS because it gave a visual representation of what we were coding and made it customizable so we could make a connection to our work. For example the photo used to the left is of a pizza made with different shapes learned in the course like an ellipse (circle) for pepperonis or a rect (rectangle) for the crust.

Intro to HTML/CSS

Intro to HTML/CSS gave insight into the skeleton of websites that we visit daily. It taught us elements of design and interaction that we see and use on any normal website. It also encourages the user to create their own website using hosts like Github. Overall this modual gave me a good understanding of classes, styles and words known to these languages and what they do to various elements of my page.

Advanced JS/Natural Simulations

Natural Simulations gave an arranged randomness to situations that would happen naturally. For example leaves falling from a tree is not an organized or predictable event. But using rand functions we could make their way down to the ground look more natural and unpredictable, like an actual tree would. In addition to other lessons we can see some other applications to this, like coding the movement of a wild animal or a paint splatter or an NPC in more advanced code in a video game.

Algorithms

Algorithms was a very useful modual this semester, it emphasized efficency when solving problems. This skill is super useful especially in computer science and math. Specific examples of this include the Towers of Hanoi which was a exercise that had you complete the puzzle of moving pegs from one tower to another in the least amount of steps. This example was helpful because it made you think about "cutting the fat" off of your solution and getting right down to the problem.

MicroBit

Micrbits were super fun. The different lessons and tutorials made interactive games, accessories and musical intruments and really opened my eyes as to how even something as simple as a tiny microbit can open a lot of possibilites. For my group's microbit we made a super cute rock, paper, scissors game which we made our own using the tutorial provided by microbit. While the original lesson made the opponents play randomly generated, ours allowed for another microbit to play.

Coding Train Challenge: Metaballs

The metaballs challenge was a very interesting challenge to attempt to refactor. Having to learn what different parts of the code do and how to change them in a meaningful and educational way really helped later in presenting because it gave me more confidence when talking about its different elements. For the actual metaballs it expanded from just colourful blobs, my group and I got the opportunity to learn about isosurfaces and their uses, which was a cool concept which is something we wouldn't have learned otherwise.

HTML Website: Origami

The origami website allowed for our team to expand our knowledge of algorithms, HTML and CSS. We got to learn how to make some cute origami art while also learning how to explain it to others, without the use of a video. Our website was visually appealling which was difficult but made our final product satisfying. I was in charge of making 2 of the pages, the flower and the dinosaur. Both of which were uniform and matched their respective themes. In addition to that I also assisted in the creation of the homepage.

Coding Train Challenge: Mathematical Rose Patterns

The mathematical rose pattern refactor was made from Sabrina's group. Their lesson touched on how the roses used trigonometry and polar coordinates. In the code provided we were given sliders to change the pattern of the rose. I changed the rgb values to be set to the sliders so when you changed the "d" and "n" values you got a different background colour.
